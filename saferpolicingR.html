Special Prosecutors and Penalizing False Reports
Would Make Pennsylvania Safer

Medical Students for Safer Policing urge Pennsylvania lawmakers to pass HB1382 and SB1198.

Communities are losing trust in police.

Many high-profile encounters between police and community members — including in Pennsylvania — have damaged our trust in police. For example, in 2018, the police were called on a group of Black women in Dover who were “golfing too slow.”

Deadly police shootings since 2015. Source: The Washington Post.

Since 2015, 111 people have been killed by police. These men and women will no longer contribute to their communities. Yet despite evidence of wrongdoing in many cases, no officers have been convicted, and few police departments have undergone reform.

We can enhance trust in police.

About two-thirds of Americans do not believe that police use the right amount of force, treat racial groups equally, or hold “bad” police accountable. Police can be safer and more effective. As future physicians, we know how essential safe communities and positive experiences with institutions are to the health of patients and communities.

In medicine, errors happen. Often deadly errors. When they do, however, we don’t blame the doctor. We examine the system. We work to prevent the circumstances that make errors in the future. Only when doctors commit gross negligence are they subject to punishment. This process improves the system. So, too, can we improve policing.

We urge the passage of HB1382 and SB1198.

Appoint special prosecutors
for deadly force cases.

Make false criminal reports based on
race a first-class misdemeanor.
